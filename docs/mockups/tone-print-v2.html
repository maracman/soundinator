Phosphor needles — one per partial, at its realised Hz (log axis). Height = final computed level (excitation × position comb × material state × body). Trail length = ring time: glassy materials leave long afterglow, felt snuffs out. The decay law is literally visible.
Body ridge — the fixed-Hz resonance profile behind everything (violin body here; a vowel when a vocal body is chosen). Explains why the same partial blooms on one note and hides on another.
Relationship arcs — tap a partial (p4 selected): relatives by true frequency ratio light up, labelled with the ratio and its cents error. Arc opacity = transfer coupling strength; inharmonicity visibly weakens arcs as ratios drift.
Excitation comb — faint underlay: the sin(nπx) node pattern of bow position. Turn Position and you watch the comb sweep and partials die at its zeros. No hidden shaping anywhere: the print is the truth.
